Instructions to the Coach

Welcome to the Comp Int Coach Portfolio.

This online form contains all of the instructions and resources required to complete your Comp Int Coach Portfolio.

The portfolio consists of an in-person assessment of you coaching during a practice sessions, a self-assessment against the Comp Int criteria, and nine evaluated tasks.  Your responses to each task must be typed into the space provided, or uploaded.   Some tasks require use of the template provided, while others can be created by the coach from scratch.  

Each task offers an objective, evaluation method, resources, templates (where applicable) and specific instructions for the task.   

Where files must be uploaded, an upload file field is provided at the end of the form.    You may navigate through the form and review all of the tasks...however a completed portfolio must include ALL fields completed and ALL tasks uploaded.

Coaches are encouraged to SAVE their work as they complete tasks.  Use the Save my progress and resume later option located conveniently at the top and bottom of each page.   The form system will provide instructions on accessing the saved form, and an email will be sent to the coach with a link to their saved form.  

When saving a form, all entered text and uploaded files are preserved, however the system will return the user to the first page.   Simply move through the pages using the NEXT button, until you reach the page where you wish to resume.

Completing the Comp Int Coach Portfolio

Peer Review

Coaches are encouraged to have a peer review your completed tasks before submitting the final version.

The purpose of the review is to see the portfolio from a new perspective, with a fresh set of eyes and understand there are different coaching techniques that can be used.

Feel free to incorporate the feedback received into your final submission.

Uploading Files

Tasks may be uploaded at any time, and new versions of a task will overwrite previously uploaded versions.   Once you reach the final page and SUBMIT the Portfolio, this is considered your final submission.

Coaches should SAVE a copy of their tasks on their own computer or storage device.

If you wish to upload a task, then return later to continue with the next task, use the SAVE and RESUME Later link AFTER uploading your file.

Once your completed portfolio has been submitted;

  • A confirmation email will be sent to you, and the Provincial Swimming office.
  • A $300 payment is required for the Portfolio evaluation.  Payment is made to the Provincial Swimming office where the club you coach with is located.
  • Once your portfolio and evaluation payment has been received, your Provincial Swimming office will notify the NCCP Administrator.   The NCCP Administrator will then assign a Comp Int Portfolio evaluator to mark your portfolio.   The Comp Int Portfolio evaluator will review your portfolio tasks.
  • If the Comp Int Portfolio evaluator deems the portfolio incomplete against the published NCCP criteria, a coach is provided ONE follow up opportunity to make the required adjustments.   Should the requested adjustments fall short of the minimum NCCP criteria, the Comp Int Portfolio evaluator will submit an incomplete evaluation to the NCCP Administrator, and a recommended set of next steps for the coach to pursue before re-submission of the Comp Int Portfolio.   A marking fee of $150 will again be due to the Provincial Swimming office for Comp Int Portfolio re-submissions.
  • When the Comp Int Portfolio evaluation is complete, the Comp Int Portfolio evaluator will submit a complete evaluation to the NCCP Administrator.  A copy of the evaluation form will be emailed to you and the Provincial Swimming office.
  • The final step of the Comp Int evaluation is an evaluation at a competition. After completing the Portfolio you will be provided with the information to complete your competition evaluation.

Assessment at a practice session

In order to complete the Comp Int Portfolio you are required to have an assessor observe you during one of your normal practices. The practice must take place with your normal group of swimmers, with an assessor that meets minimum qualifications.

Assessment Steps:

  1. Select your assessor - assessor requiremetns are listed below.
  2. Provide the assessor with a copy of the Assessor Guide (see below)
  3. Schedule a practice for the assessment to take place.
  4. Meet 30 to 60 minutes prior to the pracitce for the assessor to complete their pre-practice questions and observation. During the pre observation, make sure you outline to your assessor what age and stage your group of swimmers are.
  5. Allow the assessor to observe you coach your group of swimmers, without interruption for the duration of the practice.
  6. The assesor wiill conduct a debrief discussion with you after the practice, so ensure you have tme after the chosen practice to meet with the assessor.
  7. The assesor will complete an online form with the practice assessment results. The form will be emailed to you as a PDF document once completed.    A copy of the form will also be sent to the NCCP Administrator for record keeping.
  8. Upload the assessment PDF to your Comp Int Coach portfolio.

Although this is the first task, you can continue working on the remainder of the portfolio tasks and upload the practice assessment once completed. The tasks are not required to be completed in the order they are presented.

Select Your Assessor

An assessor will play an important role in the completion of your Fundamentals Coach certification process.

When selecting an assessor, be sure of the following;

  • Assessor is Level 2 / Comp Int certified or above with a minimum 3 years coaching experience at the Comp Int level;
  • Assessor will be available to view you coaching a full session with your regular group;
  • Assessor will be available to complete the assessment tool provided to them, and meet with you to discuss their observations in a debrief meeting;
  • Assessor will submit a summary of their feedback to the coach and complete an online assessment evaluation form.

Introduction - Self Assessment

Objective

Coaches will provide a self-assessment of their coaching competencies, using the NCCP criteria for Comp Int Coach certification.

Activity

Complete the self-assessment questions that follow.   These questions reflect the core criteria that are required for a coach to be certified at Comp Int Coach.  

Your responses will provide evaluators reviewing your portfolio a better understanding of your coaching strengths and weaknesses, and should help you to reflect on areas where you can focus self driven professional development.

Complete the 1 to 5 scale self assessment to determine how frequently you do each of the coaching activites:

1 - I never do that!

3 - Sometimes I do that, but not all the time

5 - I always do that!

Reference your Comp Int Coach Evaluation Guide for specific examples of the criteria listed below.

Support the Swimmer in Training
> I ensure the environment is safe for my swimmers and myself when planning and delivering my practice.
> My practice plans and practice delivery are structured and well organized.
> During my practices I actively move around the practice space, creating opportunities to interact with swimmers and promote learning.
Plan a Practice
> My practice plans include logistics for the practice (eg. objectives, duration of each segment of the practice, equipment requirements).
> My practice plans include appropriate activities for each segment of the practice (introduction, warm up, main-part, cool down).
> Unique activities are identified for swimmers of different age/stage.
> My practice plans include a clear emergency action plan, specific to the facility where we are training.
Analyze Performance
> During practice I actively position myself around the practice space to better view my swimmers technique.
> I am able to detect technical errors being made by my swimmers, and identify the potential causes of the error.
> I offer corrections for technical skill errors, using teaching methods best suited to the swimmers learning style.
> When offering corrections to technical skill errors, I verbally and visually check that the swimmer has understood the correction I have provided.
Support the Competitive Experience
> Before a competition, I plan and clearly communicate logistics and expectations for the competition.
> I work with my swimmers to prepare and implement a mental skills plan leading up to, and at competitions.
> I provide nutritional advice to swimmers and parents prior to competitions, and take appropriate measures to monitor swimmers nutrition during the competition.
> During a competition, I create opportunities to interact with swimmers prior to and after their race.
> I use opportunities to interact with swimmers at competitions to provide constructive and strategic feedback that will help the swimmer to improve in their next performance.
Design a Swimming Program
> I have a seasonal plan that outlines training and competition schedules.
> My seasonal plan prioritizes technical, tactical, mental and physical priorities throughout the season.
> My seasonal plan includes measures to identify swimmer progress, both in training and competition.
> My practice plans address specific priorities outlined in my season plan.
Manage a Program
> I am well organized, and communicate administrative logistics related to training and competition in an effective manner.
> I have a system to track and report on my swimmers training and competition progress throughout the season.

Objective

Coaches will demonstrate their ability to create a 16-28 week Seasonal Training Plan.

Evaluation

This task will be evaluated based on the Comp Int Coach Evaluation Guide - Design a Sport Program criteria and evidences.   Coaches should reference this information while completing the task.

  • Design a Basic Sport Program Coach Workbook
  • Guidelines for Training of Athletic Abilities and Athlete's Age
  • Swimming Canada LTAD Model
  • Swimming 201 Coach Workbook (received at Swimming 201 course)
Part 1 - Seasonal Training Plan

Create a seasonal training plan that covers 16-28 weeks of training and competitive opportunities for the group of swimmers you coach. You will need to access information from your Provincial Sport Organization, Region, Club and School Board to help you develop this plan (e.g. practice and meet schedule, holidays and school exam times, etc).

The training plan you design should be a single periodization, with one peak competition phase.

The seasonal training plan template provided is an excel file called Seasonal Training Plan Template. To complete the template, follow the coding instructions provided on the template.  

Your final seasonal plan must allow another coach to read the plan and pick up where you left off without any question as to what stroke, turn, or programming element was planned and prioritized for that week.

The key steps for a seasonal training plan can be found in the resources above titled CAC Design a Basic Sport Program Reference Book. The seasonal training plan should include the following elements:

  1. Length of the season, breaks and other planning logistics (e.g. holidays, travel, school, exams, etc.)
  2. The plan must include the three main periods of your season, preparation, competition and transition.
  3. The number of training sessions and competition sessions in your season.
  4. Major program objectives related to the specifics of: Physical, motor skills, lifestyle, mental, tactical and technical
  5. Prioritization of training through the season, and monitoring of all training areas.

Task #3 Plan Effective Practices

Objective

Coaches will demonstrate their ability to plan by:

Part 1. Planning an effective practice for each one of the three phases used in a Seasonal Training Plan.

Part 2. Planning an action plan in case of an emergency.

Evaluation

This task will be evaluated based on the Comp Int Coach Evaluation Guide - Plan a Practice criteria and evidences.   Coaches should reference this information while completing the task.

Resources

  • Key Questions for Planning a Practice
  • Key Elements in a Practice
  • Guidelines for Training Athltic Abilities and Athlete's Ages
  • Swimming 201 Coach Workbook (received at Swimming 201 course)
Part #1 - Plan a Practice

It is important for coaches to have a detailed plan for every practice coached so you can efficiently guide your swimmers.

In Task #2 you created a week plan for your swimmers in each of the three season phases, General Preparatory Phase, Specific Preparatory Phase and Competition Phase.

Using the three one week plans created in Task #2, create one detailed practice plan for a morning or afternoon session from each of the one week plans.

The selected practice shall all be from the same session in each of the three plans. (e.g. Wednesday PM from each of: General Preparatory Phase, Specific Preparatory Phase and Competition Phase)

Your practice plans should be well organized and your objectives, activities, drills etc. explained clearly enough that another coach could easily step in, run your practice in your absence and complete the practice in the time available.

Each of your practice plans should include the following:

  1. Introductory activities that review and reinforce previous learning.
  2. Drills and Activities that contribute to the development of swimming skills and athletic abilities.
  3. Drills and activities that are appropriate for the age and skill level of the swimmers you coach.
  4. Clearly explained drills and activities that match the overall objective of your practice.
  5. Drills that support the main teaching points you want to focus on for stroke technique (e.g. if your plan indicated a focus on freestyle body position, your drills should focus on the same skill).
  6. Drills and activities that minimize the risk of damage or harm to swimmers or equipment.
  7. A constructive contingency plan and strategies for dealing with unforeseen challenges.

Create your practice plans as three (3) separate documents:

General_Prep_Practice_YOURNAME

Specific_Prep_Practice_YOURNAME

Competition_Practice_YOURNAME

Objective

Coaches will demonstrate their ability to plan the three week period leading up to a specific swim meet.

Evaluation

Part #1 - Competition Plan

Using the Competition Plan Template in the resources above, create a competition plan which covers the final 21 days leading up to the first day of competition at a medium or high priority swim meet from your seasonal plan. List all the tasks you as a coach need to prepare and complete. Once you have determined the list of tasks, prioritize them by importance. Identify any individuals who may also be assisting you and their responsibilities.

Your plan should detail the specific activities you will do to address your group of swimmer's physical, technical, tactical and psychological preparation for the swim meet. Include travel arrangements, parental contact etc.

Be sure to include warm-up and cool-down procedures, group/team meetings, etc. that will be used throughout the swim meet.

Part #2 - Nutrition Advice

Proper nutrition is an important factor in your swimmers ability to perform to their fullest potential.

In your Competition Plan Template, provide details on how you will advise and monitor your swimmers nutrition and hydration within your 21 day plan, at the competition and after the competition.
